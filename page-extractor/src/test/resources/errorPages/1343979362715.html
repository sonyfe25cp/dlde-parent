    Sage-Stuffed Pork Fillet with Lentils and Scallion Dressing

    1 pork tenderloin, about 14 oz.
    Leaves from about 1 1/2 of those packages of sage, or from a large bunch
    4 slices prosciutto
    1 1/2 cups French green lentils
    1 bunch of scallions, sliced
    1 tablespoon olive oil/div>
    1 tablespoon red wine
    1/2 cup sour cream (fat free, if you like)
    1 lb can roasted bell peppers, drained and sliced (particularly good if they've been kept in a vinegary seasoning)
    Bunch of chives, chopped
    S/P

    Oven preheated to 350

    Trim the fat and the silver skin from your tenderloin. lice the tenderloin in half horizontally in the middle. ith a paring knife, pierce each tenderloin lengthwise through the middle, then rotate the knife 90 degrees and pierce again, making an x. nsert the handle of a wooden spoon into the slit and roll it around a little, opening up the pocket you've just made. nto this pocket, stuff the sage. o this on each piece of tenderloin.

    Season the tenderloin with salt and pepper, then wrap in the prosciutto slices, which will stick all on their own. n an oiled roasted pan, place the tenderloin and cook for 35 minutes. llow to rest for 5 minutes before cutting in.

    In the meantime, prepare the lentils. ook the lentils in simmering water for about 30 minutes (they'll maintain some of their bite), then drain. n a bowl, mix the scallions, oil, wine and sour cream. dd the bell peppers to the lentils. erve with the tenderloin plated on top of the lentils, a bit of scallion dressing spooned over the top, and a generous sprinkling of chives. lternately, you could mix the scallion dressing in with the lentils, which is what I suggest doing with any leftovers. njoy!
